2 - DIMENSIONAL FIGURES

A 2D (two-dimensional) shape can be defined as a plane figure that can be drawn on a flat surface. It has only two dimensions – length and width, with no thickness or depth. Some of the basic 2D shapes are Rectangle, Square, Circle, Triangle, Trapezium, Paralleleogram, Rhombus, and Ellipse.

3 - DIMENSIONAL FIGURES

A three-dimensional shape can be defined as a solid
figure or an object or shape that has three dimensions –
length, width and height. Unlike two-dimensional shapes,
three-dimensional shapes have thickness or depth Some
of the basic 3D shapes are Cuboid, Cube, Cylinder, Cone,
Frustum of Cone, Sphere, Hemisphere, and Prism..
